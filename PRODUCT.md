# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Hiring managers and recruiters evaluating fit for full-time roles, and founders looking for contract or MVP help. The homepage speaks to both with equal weight — neither audience is secondary.

## Product Purpose

Make Luis easy to hire: clear stack, experience, and fit at a glance. Success is a page he’s proud to send — the link that represents him well.

## Positioning

Full-stack engineer who takes products from idea to launch.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: **Book a call** (Cal.com) and **Say Hi** (email) are co-equal primary actions. Soft fallback for visitors not ready to contact: follow on X, LinkedIn, or GitHub.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: Full stack engineer who takes products from idea to launch.
- Belief ladder: Personable and clear → solid experience → building something interesting now → reach out.
- Proof on hand: BuildShip role write-up; Video Journal demo and highlights; Screen Mockups first-payout story (TrustMRR); resume PDF; tech stack logos. No extra testimonials or press beyond what’s already on the site.

## Brand Personality

**Curious · Shipping · Grounded.** Builder energy — curious, shipping, slightly scrappy — not quiet CV polish and not hype. Voice stays direct and human; credentials support the story instead of leading it.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS landing pages (hero metric strips, gradient CTAs, feature card grids). Over-designed designer-portfolio theater (heavy animation, case-study fluff). Dense recruiter-template résumés that feel corporate and lifeless.

## Design Principles

- **Hireable at a glance** — a scanning visitor should leave with stack, experience, and fit without a scavenger hunt.
- **Prove by shipping** — show real products, demos, and the first-payout story before asking for trust.
- **Person first, then proof** — warm clarity opens the door; credentials close it.
- **Tight single-column density** — résumé-like signal density without corporate template energy.
- **Co-equal outreach** — booking and email stay equally easy; social is the soft path for lurkers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Sensible defaults: workable contrast, keyboard access, and respect for reduced motion. No formal WCAG certification target.
